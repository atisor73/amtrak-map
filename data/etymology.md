**Borealis**
    - Borealis is latin for Northern, which travels north from Chicago to the Twin Cities. "The Borealis is one of three state-supported routes with a locally owned name, along with Northern California’s Capitol Corridor service and the Boston-to-Maine Downeaster." **Sunset Limited**
    - The Amtrak Sunset Limited is named for its historic westerly route from New Orleans to Los Angeles—often referred to as the "Sunset Route"—designed to catch sunsets over the southwestern desert, coupled with the term "limited" which historically signified a premier, high-speed train making fewer stops.

**Cascades**
    - The Amtrak Cascades is named after the Cascade Mountain range that the rail line parallels throughout the Pacific Northwest. Running between Vancouver, British Columbia, and Eugene, Oregon, this service is so-called because its route offers passengers views of the snow-clad summits of the Cascade Mountains

**California Zephyr**
    - The Amtrak California Zephyr is named after the historic, premier passenger train service that operated between Chicago and San Francisco from 1949 to 1970, which was famed for its scenic, leisurely route through the Rockies and Sierra Nevadas. The original "Zephyr" name comes from Zephyrus, the Greek god of the west wind, symbolizing speed and, later, the comfortable, scenic journey west. (https://media.amtrak.com/2024/03/happy-anniversary-california-zephyr/#:~:text=The%20California%20Zephyr%20was%20named,to%20regularly%20use%20dome%20cars.)

**Downeaster**
    - The Amtrak Downeaster is named after the coastal region of Maine, historically referred to as "Down East". The term originates from 19th-century sailing, where in the warm months, winds along the coast blow southwest, meaning ships traveling from Boston to Maine had to sail downwind to go East. It now represents the train’s service connecting Boston, Massachusetts, to various coastal towns in Maine. **Cardinal**
    - The Amtrak Cardinal (operating between New York and Chicago) was renamed from the James Whitcomb Riley on October 30, 1977. It is named after the northern cardinal, which is the state bird for many of the states along its route, including Virginia, West Virginia, Ohio, and Illinois

The name originally signified speed, referencing the poem's "swift of foot" Native American character

**Crescent**
The Amtrak Crescent is named after the historic "Crescent Limited" train operated by the Southern Railway between New York and New Orleans. The name refers to the "Crescent City" a popular nickname for New Orleans due to its location along the bend of the Mississippi River.
![](/images/new_orleans_crescent_city.jpg)

**Silver Meteor**
The Amtrak Silver Meteor is named after its 1939 debut as a high-speed, stainless-steel (silver) diesel streamliner operated by the Seaboard Air Line Railroad (SAL) to Florida. The name, chosen via contest, highlighted the train's modern, fast, and sleek ("meteor") design, a legacy continued by Amtrak since 1971.
"Silver": Refers to the shining, unpainted stainless steel construction of the cars, which were manufactured by the Budd Company.
"Meteor": Symbolized speed and modernity during its 1939 launch, as it was the first diesel-powered streamliner to Florida.
Heritage: The name was selected from 76,000 contest entries and was retained by Amtrak to honor the original SAL service.

**Palmetto**
Amtrak's Palmetto (running between New York and Savannah) is named as a nod to the Sabal palmetto, the state tree of South Carolina. The name honors a historic Atlantic Coast Line Railroad route from 1909

**Piedmont**
The Amtrak Piedmont is named after the Piedmont plateau region of the eastern United States (specifically North Carolina), which translates to "at the foot of the mountain" from Latin. It serves the region between Raleigh and Charlotte, which is nestled between the coast and the Appalachian Mountains.


**Berkshire Flyer**
The Amtrak Berkshire Flyer is named for its destination, the Berkshire mountains in Western Massachusetts, offering direct, fast weekend service from New York City. Destination: The train services the Pittsfield, MA area, which is in the heart of the Berkshires.

**Adirondack**
The Amtrak Adirondack train is named after the Adirondack Mountains in upstate New York, as its route runs along the eastern edge of this region between New York City, Albany, and Montreal. The name highlights the scenic journey through the Adirondack region, which is famous for its wild, natural beauty. 
Origin of Name: The name "Adirondack" is derived from a Mohawk word, atirǫ́ ·taks, which translates to "tree eaters," originally a term used to refer to Algonquian people.

**Keystone Service**
    - Amtrak's Keystone Service is named after the "Keystone State" (Pennsylvania), as the route travels along the historic Keystone Corridor between New York, Philadelphia, and Harrisburg.


**Capitol Corridor**
    - The Capitol Corridor Amtrak service is named for connecting California's current capital (Sacramento) and the original capital (San Jose)

**Coast Starlight**
    - The Amtrak Coast Starlight is named as a tribute to two former Southern Pacific Railroad trains that operated on the same West Coast route: the Coast Daylight (daytime service) and the Starlight (overnight service). The name combines these legacies to represent the premier, scenic service connecting Seattle and Los Angeles.
